Observe the following interesting results when the base is 10.
100  =  1;   101  =  10;   102  =  10  ×  10  =  100;
    (One zero)   (Two zeros)
103  =  10  ×  10  ×  10  =  1000; 104=10×10×10×10=10000;
(Three zeros) (Four zeros)
In general, to evaluate any exponent with base 10, we write the number 1, followed by as many zeros as is the exponent.
108  =  100,000,000;
(eight zeros)
With this observation, it becomes easy to multiply a whole number by a base ten number.
We know that to multiply a number by 10, we merely have to attach a zero to the right of the number.
5  ×  10  =  50;
23  ×  10  =  230;
705  ×  10  =  7050;
Similarly, if a whole number is multiplied by 10n, where n is a whole number, then we attach n zeros to the right of the number;
Thus,
156  ×  103
=
56,000
Thus,
125  ×  105
=
12,500,000
Since division is the inverse of multiplication, dividing a number by 10 or 100 or 1000,.... will eliminate the last 1 or 2 or 3,....zeros on the right of the number;
So,
32500  ÷  102
=
3250
So,
32500  ÷  102
=
325
To multiply or divide an integer by 10n , where n is a whole number:   232 × 104
Step 1:
Identify the exponent of 10.
232 × 104
Step 2:
If the number is to be multiplied, write the number and attach as many zeros to its right as the exponent of 10.
2,320,000
Step 3:
If the number is to be divided, eliminate the same number of digits from the right of the number as the exponent. The value eliminated is the remainder.
32542 ÷ 102 = 325 42
325 is Quotient and 42 is Remainder or 325 R 42
