The symbol to indicate addition is “+” and is read as “plus”.
The result of an addition is called the sum.
The numbers being added are called addends or terms.
8 + 3 = 11 is read "8 plus 3 equals 11"
8 and 3 are the addends or terms, and 11 is the sum
The symbol for subtraction is “–” ( read : “minus”).
When one number is subtracted from the other the result is called the difference.
The numbers involved in this subtraction are called the minuend and the subtrahend.
12 – 3 = 9 is read as "12 minus 3 equals 9"
12 – 3 = 9 is read as "12 is the minuend
12 – 3 = 9 is read as "3 is the subtrahend
12 – 3 = 9 is read as "and 9 is the difference.

Thus, we observe that addition and subtraction represent inverse operations.
For each addition fact, we can write two related subtraction facts.
For instance, 5 + 4 = 9 represents two subtraction facts:  9 – 4 = 5   and   9 – 5 = 4.
