We cannot divide 0 by 0.
It is important to note that we cannot divide any non-zero whole number by zero.
For example, let us try to divide 7 by 0.
Dividing 7 by 0 is the same as finding the missing factor in the product
0×?=7.
No whole number can be found to meet this requirement 0×?=7.
We say that division by zero is undefined.
However, we can divide zero by any non-zero whole number.
Since,
11×0=0,
we can say
0÷11=0
10×9=0,
we can say
0÷91=0
Observe that the quotient in each case is zero.
When zero is divided by any non-zero number, the quotient is always zero.
Multiplication facts, such as 3×1=3 and 12×1=12, give rise to very special types of division facts.

When any number is divided by 1, the quotient is the number itself:  8÷1=8.
When a non-zero number is divided by itself, the quotient is always 1:  4÷4=1.
The division facts considered so far involved small numbers so the quotient could be obtained either by inspection or by the method of repeated subtraction.
But these methods are not very convenient to apply in every problem of division, such as finding the quotient of 9,360÷45.
Moreover, division does not always involve factors (or exact divisors). A more general method of performing division is commonly called the method of long division or the division algorithm and is illustrated below.
Let us divide 69 by 3.
●
Divide the left most place (tens place in this case). 6 tens ÷ 3 = 2 tens.
●
Do subtraction in the tens places, and bring the next digit down from the dividend.
●
Divide in the next place(ones place in this case): 9 ones ÷ 3 = 3 ones.
●
Continue the process until all the digits in the dividend have been used.
So, 69÷3=23.
Not all division problems have a zero remainder.
Divide 2,718 by 5
●
2 (the left most digit in the dividend) cannot be divided by 5; so, we consider the group 27, the first two digits from the left.
●
5 can go into 27 only 5 times.
●
Bring down the next digit in the dividend, and write it next to the remainder 2 to get the number 21.
●
Now, divide 21 by 5. Continue the process until all the digits in the dividend have been used.
Symbolically, we write2718÷5=543R3
